VISION


Psycle's vision is to strive to create a just Bicycle world; where people of all ages are empowered and engaged to foster progressive participation, encourage their good health and in effect serve to save our MOTHER EARTH. We envision a Psycle where riding a bicycle is always safe and convenient for everyone, where bicycling is the top choice for recreation and everyday trips, and where the benefits of bicycling are experienced and valued by all people in our state.The East Bay is a leader for innovative and accessible bikeways and streets that meet the needs of all users.
Bicycling is a mainstream, comfortable and safe choice for people of all cultures, ages, abilities, and backgrounds. Bicycles are well integrated into the transportation system and are a key part of our thriving communities.



MISSION


PSYCLE's MOTTO : ‘’ ALL FOR CYCLING : CYCLES FOR ALL ‘’ PSYCLE to work towards meeting ever increasing needs and aspirations of all bicycle lovers and concurrently assist in building Ecologically Sustainable Transport and Communities based on cohesive capacity through close, continuous coordination , while ensuring desired credibility and due care.We are committed to sharing our passion and knowledge for bicycles and the lifestyle through Cycling products and straightforward service that works for you.We believe cycling will be commonplace, accessible, and reliable. Our culture will be deeply infused with a knowledge of and passion for bicycles. Active transportation will transform our cities into healthy, lively communities.

GOAL


Increase the number of people that exercise daily by providing safe biking experiences for citizens of all ages and levels of ability. of people bicycling for everyday transportation purposes such as commuting to work, to school and running errands. Enhance community connections to neighborhoods, parks, schools, businesses, retail and dining, and government facilities. This goal is also for casual bike riders that may not be comfortable riding among automobile or truck traffic. These types of riders account for 60% of the bicycling population, and require improved infrastructure or residential streets with low traffic and speed limits to make connections within the community
